Garbage in Cyprus today

Cyprus produced 543,000 tonnes of solid waste in 2020, which was about 5% less then the year before.

Of the total 543,000 tonnes of solid waste, 462,000 tonnes was managed with 78.8 per cent disposed in landfills, 18.3 per cent recycled, 1.2 per cent used for compost purposes and 1.7 per cent for energy recovery.

This puts it to around 600kg of waste per person, which makes it the 5th worst in the EU, after:

  1. Denmark (845 kg)
  2. Luxembourg (790 kg)
  3. Malta (643 kg)
  4. and Germany (632 kg)

According to data from Green Dot, in 2021 12,514 tonnes of PMD, 9,889 tonnes of paper and 5,963 tonnes of glass were recycled, a total of 29,727 tonnes. This was up on the 27,319 tonnes of waste recycled the previous year.

According to a survey in Cyprus, 73 per cent of the households are buying more food than the required amount and 85 per cent of households said that when preparing or ordering food, there is always a surplus. According to the survey, the average household throws 23 per cent of its weekly purchases in food, vegetables and fruits in the trash.
